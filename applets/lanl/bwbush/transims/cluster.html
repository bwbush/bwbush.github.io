Clustering of Cellular Automaton Traffic Microsimulation Output

Overview

The TRANSIMS microsimulation uses a cellular automata approach to provide the computational speed necessary to simulate an entire region at the individual traveler level. The cellular automata technique provides a means to simulate large numbers of vehicles and maintain a fast execution speed. Each link in the transportation network is divided into a finite number of cells. At each timestep of the simulation, each cell is examined for a vehicle occupant. If a vehicle is present in the cell, the vehicle may be advanced to another cell using a simple rule set. Increasing the fidelity by decreasing the cell size, adding vehicle attributes, and expanding the rule set results in slower computational speed. The fidelity and performance limits of the cellular automata microsimulation are evaluated to establish the computational detail required to support the fidelity necessary to meet analysis requirements.

The applet below is a simple example of how the output from a cellular automaton traffic microsimulation can be classified using an automatic clustering algorithm.

Instuctions:

Press "+" (or "-") to add (or remove) vehicles from the roadway. [You may have to click the mouse on the image first, in order to give the canvas the keyboard focus.]

Key to Left Side

  • Each pixel represents a piece of roadway 7.5 meters long for 1 second.
  • The rows of the image represent the roadway at a fixed time.
  • The columns of the image represent fixed locations on the roadway.
  • Vehicles travel from left to right.
  • Vehicles that leave the right side of the roadway reenter on the left side.
  • The following color scheme is used to display vehicles:
    black no vehicle
    white vehicle traveling 0.0 m/s
    red vehicle traveling 7.5 m/s
    orange vehicle traveling 15.0 m/s
    yellow vehicle traveling 22.5 m/s
    green vehicle traveling 30.0 m/s
    blue vehicle traveling 37.5 m/s

The cellular automaton rules are described here.

Key to Right Side

  • Each group of four pixels represents a piece of roadway 30 meters long for 1 second.
  • The rows of the image represent the roadway at a fixed time.
  • The columns of the image represent fixed locations on the roadway.
  • Segments of the roadway are classified according to an automatic clustering algorithm that is sensistive to the presence, speed, and dispersion among vehicles.
